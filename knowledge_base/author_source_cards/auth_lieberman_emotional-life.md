## Claim (paraphrased principle)

Infants and young children have a rich emotional life, and their behaviour is a form of
communication. Warm, attuned, predictable responses to a child's signals build emotional
security and support the early foundations of self-regulation.

## Practical implication

Respond to your baby's cues with comfort and warmth, gently name what they might be
feeling, and keep daily routines predictable. Treat distress as communication, not
misbehaviour.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

This expresses responsive caregiving and early co-regulation (Silver) and aligns with the
WHO Nurturing Care Framework.

## Allowed wording

- "responding warmly to your baby's signals supports emotional security"
- "comfort and predictable routines help your baby feel safe"

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Not therapy; does not prevent or treat mental-health conditions.
- Claim limit: supports the everyday foundation of emotional regulation, nothing more.

## Safety note

Lieberman's book centres on toddlers; the responsive-caregiving principle applies from
infancy. If a parent is worried about their child's emotional wellbeing or their own,
encourage professional support.
