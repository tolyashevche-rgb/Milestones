## Claim (paraphrased principle)

Noticing a possible developmental concern is only one part of a professional care process. Useful
next steps include documenting the caregiver’s observations clearly, sharing them with an
appropriate professional, and following what happens next. An app can support preparation and
continuity, but it cannot perform the professional interpretation or close the loop itself.

## Practical implication

The specialist-summary and observation-history features should remain factual, dated, concise,
and easy for the caregiver to correct. They can show what was observed, what remains uncertain,
what worries the caregiver, and what they want to ask. They must not write a clinical impression,
recommend a service as if eligibility were known, or suggest that saving a note counts as care.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

This is a workflow and safety-boundary card, not an infant-development mechanism. Poon’s
co-edited professional handbook covers detection, communication with families, monitoring,
collaboration, and practical developmental-behavioral care. Milestones uses only that process
shape; current AAP/CDC guidance remains the authority for monitoring and screening distinctions.

## Allowed wording

- “Here is a summary of what you observed and what you want to discuss.”
- “You can edit this note before sharing it with a professional.”
- “Saving an observation helps preserve context; it is not a clinical conclusion.”
- “If the concern remains, note what changed and bring it to the next conversation.”

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Do not generate a clinical impression, suspected condition, urgency level, referral order,
  treatment plan, prognosis, or service-eligibility conclusion.
- Do not rewrite caregiver observations into stronger medical language or present an app-generated
  summary as a clinician’s note.
- Do not claim that a saved note, repeated observation, exported summary, or scheduled reminder
  completes screening, referral, evaluation, follow-up, or care coordination.
- Do not promise access to services, a diagnosis, early-intervention eligibility, or a particular
  developmental outcome.
- Do not erase uncertainty or label “no new concern” as proof that no concern exists.
- Claim limit: supports accurate preparation and continuity only; all interpretation, screening,
  referral, diagnosis, and care decisions remain with qualified people and local systems.

## Safety note

Documentation must never become a reason to wait when a caregiver reports loss of skills, an
urgent health issue, or immediate danger. The app should direct the caregiver to appropriate
professional or emergency help according to its existing safety rules.
