## Claim (paraphrased principle)

Classic child-development theorists converge on a practical point: children learn through
active, relationship-based experience suited to their current stage, not by being pushed
ahead of it. Mooney's contribution is to translate these theories into everyday caregiving
language.

## Practical implication

Follow the child's current stage and interests; offer hands-on, relational experiences
rather than drills. Use this as background reasoning for *why* responsive, play-based
activities are chosen — not as a source of specific exercises.
