## Claim (paraphrased principle)

Luria is useful only as historical internal context against reducing a complex ability to one
isolated brain location. His clinical neuropsychology and language-development theory do not
provide a parent-facing recommendation for an infant app, and no Milestones activity or
interpretation should be derived from them.

## Practical implication

There is no direct caregiver action from this card. Its internal use is a review question: does
the product accidentally explain one observable infant behavior through a specific brain area,
lesion, functional unit, or causal language claim? Parent-facing communication and play guidance
must instead come from current infant-specific sources such as WHO responsive caregiving.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

This card does not establish a current infant mechanism. It records a historical systems-oriented
perspective and, more importantly, its limits. *The Working Brain* is a clinical
neuropsychological text with substantial lesion-based material; that evidence cannot be
translated into conclusions about a healthy individual infant from an app observation.

## Allowed wording

- “Luria is retained as historical internal context, not as a source of infant advice.”
- “Complex behavior should not be reduced to a single brain-area explanation.”
- “No parent-facing recommendation or activity is derived from this card.”

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Do not infer brain location, lesion, dysfunction, immaturity, delay, or diagnosis from an
  infant’s observed behavior or a Milestones answer.
- Do not claim that speech or adult verbal instruction causes, unlocks, organizes, or accelerates
  an infant’s thinking or self-regulation.
- Do not transfer findings from adults or children with neurological injuries directly to healthy
  infants.
- Do not use functional units, cortical zones, hemispheres, localization, or “higher mental
  functions” as a simplified parent-facing explanation.
- Do not imply that more talking, earlier words, or a particular exercise improves intelligence
  or rewires the brain.
- Claim limit: historical methodology guardrail only; it supplies no infant milestone, activity,
  diagnosis, treatment, prognosis, or developmental outcome claim.

## Safety note

Language concerns and unusual loss of skills require qualified clinical assessment, not
neuropsychological interpretation by the app. The product must remain descriptive and follow its
existing “when to discuss” guidance without naming a brain cause.
