## Claim (paraphrased principle)

The only safe reusable form is simple: when the baby is awake, offer a clear, supervised floor
space, a nearby safe object or caregiver face, and time for self-initiated movement. Talking or
singing can make the shared moment pleasant. This form is supported by current WHO/AAP guidance;
*Smart Start* is inspiration only and is not evidence that an exercise changes the brain,
coordination, speech, learning, reflexes, muscle tone, or future ability.

## Practical implication

Milestones may use a brief, infant-led floor-play setup with an easy stop: place the baby in a safe
awake position appropriate for them, stay within reach, let them choose whether and how to move,
and stop when they turn away, tire, cry, stiffen, or need care. No Sasse exercise is copied. The
caregiver does not pull, lift, spin, stretch, place, or train the baby into a movement.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

WHO recommends varied physical activity several times a day for infants, particularly interactive
floor-based play, with awake tummy time spread through the day for babies who are not yet mobile.
AAP guidance similarly keeps tummy time awake and supervised. Those sources provide the evidence
and safety boundary. The book contributes only the broad idea that movement can be embedded in
play, song, and ordinary interaction.

## Allowed wording

- “Offer a safe floor space and let the baby choose the movement.”
- “Stay close, talk or sing, and follow the baby’s cues.”
- “Tummy time is only while awake and continuously supervised.”
- “Inspired by the playful setup—not the developmental claims—in Margaret Sassé’s *Smart Start*.”

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Do not copy or recommend the book’s pull-ups, push-ups, passive limb exercises, forced
  stretching, pelvis lifting, hand-walking, assisted standing or walking, therapy-ball/pouffe
  rolling, blanket swinging, spinning, tumbling, ladders, stairs, trapezes, or command exercises.
- Do not claim tummy time, crawling, creeping, cross-pattern movement, repetition, massage,
  rhythm, vestibular stimulation, or any exercise forms brain pathways, integrates senses,
  inhibits primitive reflexes, develops speech or mathematics, improves intelligence, or prevents
  later learning difficulty.
- Do not say crawling is required, bottom shuffling is a problem, early walking is risky, or a
  missed movement stage must be corrected or “caught up”.
- Do not use “critical period”, “what you don’t use, you lose”, frequency/intensity/duration,
  “more stimulation”, or future school-performance pressure.
- Do not diagnose or treat muscle tone, reflexes, food sensitivity, ear problems, sensory
  integration, posture, vision, asymmetry, or developmental delay through an activity.
- Do not use flash cards or word-visualisation drills from the book.
- Claim limit: safe play-form inspiration only; no exercise, dose, motor sequence, medical claim,
  developmental correction, brain claim, prediction, or guaranteed outcome.

## Safety note

Sleep is always on the back in a safe sleep space; floor/tummy play is only awake and continuously
supervised. Never force a position or movement, lift or pull a baby by the hands or arms, place a
baby on raised or unstable equipment, or continue through distress or fatigue. Pain, marked
asymmetry, loss of skills, unusual stiffness/floppiness, or caregiver concern belongs with a
qualified health professional, not an exercise program.
