## Claim (paraphrased principle)

Bernstein is useful as historical internal context for understanding movement as coordinated,
adaptive, and dependent on the task and conditions rather than as one rigid ideal pattern. This
does not by itself validate an infant activity, motor milestone interpretation, or exercise dose.

## Practical implication

There is no direct caregiver action from this card. Current WHO infant guidance—not Bernstein—
sets the parent-facing boundary for safe, varied, supervised floor-based activity. The card may
only prompt internal reviewers to avoid “perfect movement” language and unsupported claims that
repetition, variation, or a specific setup teaches or accelerates a motor skill.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

Later literature identifies Bernstein’s work as foundational to theories of motor control,
sensory feedback, coordination, and skill acquisition. That makes it useful for conceptual
orientation, but it is not current infant-specific intervention evidence. Milestones therefore
keeps the historical theory separate from its WHO-based practical and safety guidance.

## Allowed wording

- “Bernstein is retained as historical internal motor-control context.”
- “Infant movement need not be described as one perfect pattern.”
- “No activity, dose, or developmental conclusion is derived from this card.”

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Do not claim that varied practice, repetition, feedback, or a specific game accelerates motor
  development, produces dexterity, or builds coordination in an infant.
- Do not grade movement as efficient, inefficient, correct, incorrect, normal, abnormal, mature,
  or immature using Bernstein’s theory.
- Do not prescribe passive limb manipulation, forced positions, restraint, fatigue, or repeated
  practice after the infant turns away or becomes distressed.
- Do not infer a neurological cause, motor disorder, prognosis, or treatment need from an app
  observation.
- Do not replace WHO age-appropriate activity and safety guidance with a historical theory.
- Claim limit: conceptual guardrail only; it supplies no infant activity, dose, milestone window,
  assessment, treatment, or outcome claim.

## Safety note

Movement opportunities must remain awake, supervised, age-appropriate, and responsive to the
baby’s cues. Never force a position or movement. Pain, marked asymmetry, loss of skills, or a
caregiver concern belongs with a qualified health professional rather than an in-app motor theory.
