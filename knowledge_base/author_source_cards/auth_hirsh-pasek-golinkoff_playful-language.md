## Claim (paraphrased principle)

Early language grows through warm, responsive, back-and-forth interaction and playful
engagement — a conversational duet — far more than through flashcards, drills, or screen
time. The quality and contingency of interaction matter most.

## Practical implication

Talk with your baby, not just at them: follow their gaze and sounds, take turns, narrate
everyday moments, and keep it playful. Respond to coos and babbles as if they were part
of a conversation.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

This is the serve-and-return mechanism applied to language, and it aligns with CDC
communication milestones (turn-taking, responding to voices, early sounds).

## Allowed wording

- "playful back-and-forth talk supports early communication"
- "responding to your baby's sounds helps language grow"

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Not "makes your baby talk early" or "makes your baby smarter".
- Claim limit: supports the prerequisites of communication; it does not guarantee an
  outcome and does not treat a language delay.

## Safety note

Routine, low-risk. If a parent is concerned about hearing or very limited sounds,
route to the "Ask" tab and a professional.
