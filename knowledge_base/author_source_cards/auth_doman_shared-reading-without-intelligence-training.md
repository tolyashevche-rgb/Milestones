## Claim (paraphrased principle)

The only infant-safe reusable form is enjoyable shared reading without a teaching target. A
caregiver may let the baby look at, touch, mouth, or turn a sturdy book, talk about one picture,
pause, and follow the baby's interest. Current AAP guidance supports shared reading from infancy
as a relationship-rich routine; Doman's cards and programs are not evidence that a baby can be
taught to read or calculate, that stimulation grows the brain, or that intelligence can be
multiplied.

## Practical implication

Offer an age-safe book during an ordinary awake moment, let the baby explore it in their own way,
name or describe what catches their attention, and stop or switch activities when they turn away
or need care. There is no card deck, lesson, correct answer, word recognition, quantity task,
daily quota, or expected progress. A baby chewing a board book or listening briefly is already
participating; this is shared time, not early academic instruction.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

The 2024 AAP literacy report emphasizes joyful, developmentally appropriate shared reading,
language-rich interaction, affection, and responsiveness beginning in infancy. WHO nurturing care
and Harvard serve-and-return reinforce following the child's signals. These current sources
provide the mechanism and safety boundary. Doman contributes no accepted evidence for reading,
math, IQ, brain-growth, neurological-organization, or treatment claims.

## Allowed wording

- "Share a sturdy book and follow what interests the baby."
- "Name one picture, pause, and let touching, mouthing, looking away, or page turning guide you."
- "The goal is a pleasant shared moment, not teaching the baby to read."
- "Shared reading is supported by current guidance; Doman's intelligence-training claims are not."

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Do not say an infant reads words, understands print, recognizes quantities, performs arithmetic,
  has instant math, or learns reading more naturally or easily than speech.
- Do not prescribe word cards, dot cards, "bits of intelligence", encyclopedic facts, equations,
  card colours or dimensions, distraction-free rooms, material sequences, or commercial courses.
- Do not use one-second presentations, rapid input, card sets, rotations, retirements, daily
  sessions, frequency/intensity/duration, or any dose, schedule, curriculum, or progress level.
- Do not ask the baby to choose an answer, demonstrate recognition, repeat a word, solve a problem,
  perform for another adult, or infer reading, number sense, memory, boredom, or ability from gaze,
  reaching, touching, smiling, vocalizing, or chance selection.
- Do not claim cards, reading, math, facts, sensory input, or movement increase IQ, intelligence,
  knowledge, curiosity, concentration, brain size, brain growth, neurological organization,
  school readiness, later achievement, superiority, genius, or unlimited potential.
- Do not use "use it or lose it", "school-proof", lightning-speed learning, IQ 200, a brain age
  ahead of chronological age, or birth-to-six as a critical, last-chance, or optimal deadline.
- Do not use hugs, kisses, praise, food, approval, or the words "bright" and "smart" as payment or
  reinforcement for attending to cards or producing a correct response.
- Do not transfer theories or practices from brain injury to typically developing babies. Do not
  recommend patterning, passive limb sequences, forced crawling or creeping, hanging, brachiation,
  masking/rebreathing, oxygen manipulation, sensory bombardment, infant swimming, or accelerated
  physical stages.
- Do not claim to treat, normalize, cure, or improve brain injury, cerebral palsy, Down syndrome,
  seizures, developmental delay, learning difficulty, mobility, vision, hearing, or any condition.
- Do not privilege mothers as natural or full-time teachers, assign care by gender, or blame a
  caregiver's work, time, knowledge, enthusiasm, consistency, materials, pace, or missed sessions.
- Claim limit: optional enjoyable shared-reading form only; no Doman method, cards, dose, early
  academics, test, neurological mechanism, treatment, prediction, intelligence, or outcome claim.

## Safety note

Use an intact age-appropriate book without loose or sharp parts and supervise mouthing. Keep
reading brief and responsive; never hold a card or object beyond reach to secure attention, force
gaze or posture, remove ordinary sensory input, or continue through distress or fatigue. Never
restrict breathing or movement, force a developmental position, or substitute a home program for
clinical assessment or therapy. Developmental, neurological, vision, hearing, movement, or
learning concerns belong with qualified professionals using current evidence-based care.

  199-page PDF was searched. PDF pages 54, 70, 103, 118, and 168 were rendered and checked
  visually; they confirm IQ 200+, accelerated brain age, use-it-or-lose-it brain growth,
  instruction from birth, one-second cards, multiple daily sessions, and affection attached to
  lesson completion.

  reading claims and is neither independent evidence nor an authored clinical guideline.
