## Claim (paraphrased principle)

Give the baby unhurried time and a safe space to move on their own initiative, rather
than placing them into positions they cannot yet get into or out of by themselves. The
caregiver provides safety and attention, not correction.

## Practical implication

Offer supervised floor time and let the baby try movements at their own pace. Avoid
propping into sitting or standing they cannot reach themselves; follow the baby's lead.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

Self-initiated, supervised floor play is sensory-motor exploration: the baby practises
head, trunk and limb control through their own attempts. This aligns with the WHO view
that motor milestones have windows and vary between healthy children.

## Allowed wording

- "aligned with respectful, self-initiated movement (Pikler-inspired)"
- "give your baby time and safe space to try movements themselves"

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Not "the Pikler method guarantees motor development" or "makes your baby walk sooner".
- Claim limit: supports movement practice and observation; does not force or accelerate
  milestones.

## Safety note

Only awake, supervised floor play. Never use prone/tummy play for sleep.
