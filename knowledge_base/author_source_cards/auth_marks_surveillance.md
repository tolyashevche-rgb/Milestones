## Claim (paraphrased principle)

Routine developmental surveillance — combining parent concerns, milestone observation and
periodic validated screening — is how possible delays are noticed early and referred. It is
an ongoing process, not a single pass/fail test, and it is professional-facing.

## Practical implication

For this product: reinforces the "monitor and, if concerned, discuss with a professional"
boundary. It informs *when* to suggest a conversation, never a verdict about the child.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

Boundary card — no Silver mechanism. It maps to a Gold guideline (AAP surveillance /
screening) and governs the product's referral and "when to discuss" language only.

## Allowed wording

- "aligned with routine developmental monitoring (AAP)"
- "if you have concerns, the guidance is to discuss them with a professional"

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Not a screening or diagnostic tool; never "your child screened positive / failed".
- Claim limit: supports monitoring and a timely professional conversation; does not screen,
  score or diagnose.

## Safety note
