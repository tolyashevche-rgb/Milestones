## Claim (paraphrased principle)

A parent's observations and concerns about their child's development are meaningful and,
when gathered systematically, help identify children who may benefit from professional
follow-up. Monitoring is an ongoing process over time, not a single pass-or-fail test.

## Practical implication

Take parent worries seriously and invite parents to note what they observe. When a
concern persists, help them prepare a calm conversation with a professional, rather than
offering reassurance only.

## Allowed wording

- "your observations and concerns are worth noting and discussing"
- "if this continues to worry you, it is worth raising with a professional"

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Never a diagnosis, score, percentile, or "your child is behind".
- Claim limit: supports monitoring and the decision to seek professional input; it is not
  a screening result.
