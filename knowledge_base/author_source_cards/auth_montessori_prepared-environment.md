## Claim (paraphrased principle)

The usable Montessori idea is environmental, not clinical: prepare a simple, safe space with an
age-appropriate object the child can notice or explore, observe what attracts the child, and avoid
taking over the action. For Milestones this is inspiration for the form of play only.

## Practical implication

Place one safe object within comfortable reach during supported floor play. If needed, show one
slow action, then pause and let the child look, reach, repeat, or stop. Adjust the object or position
to the child instead of turning the moment into a task the child must complete.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

A clear environment and a pause for the child’s response can create an opportunity for
sensory-motor exploration and child-led play. WHO nurturing care provides the responsive-care and
early-learning boundary. Montessori contributes a prepared-environment form; the method itself is
not evidence that an activity changes developmental timing or outcomes.

## Allowed wording

- “Inspired by Montessori’s prepared-environment approach.”
- “Offer one safe object, show less, and leave room for the child’s own response.”
- “A simple setup can make everyday exploration easier.”

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Do not say a Montessori setup increases intelligence, produces concentration, builds
  independence earlier, accelerates milestones, or is superior to ordinary responsive play.
- Do not turn sensitive periods into deadlines or imply that a missed period causes lost ability.
- Do not use the method as proof, treatment, assessment, or a reason to withhold adult support.
- Claim limit: inspires an accessible play setup; it does not predict or alter developmental pace.

## Safety note

Use only objects too large to choke on, without loose parts, sharp edges, cords, or unstable
supports. Keep the child in a developmentally safe position with awake adult supervision. Stop if
the child turns away, cries, stiffens, coughs, or becomes tired or frustrated. Child-led never
means unattended.
