## Claim (paraphrased principle)

Developmental monitoring is an ongoing process of noticing a child’s development, listening to
caregiver concerns, and keeping a useful history. Developmental screening is a separate, more
formal process using a validated tool at recommended ages or when concerns arise. A milestone
checklist or the Milestones app must not present itself as that screening test.

## Practical implication

Milestones may help a caregiver record what they currently observe and prepare a concise summary
for a health professional. It should identify uncertainty honestly, invite discussion when a
caregiver has a concern, and preserve observations over time. It must not calculate screening
risk, interpret a missed item as a disorder, or make “all clear” promises.

## Why it maps to the mechanism

This is a clinical boundary card, not an activity mechanism. The AAP report co-authored by Macias
separates ongoing surveillance from standardized screening and places evaluation, referral, and
follow-up in professional care. The CDC’s current parent guidance likewise distinguishes informal
milestone monitoring from formal screening by trained professionals using validated tools.

## Allowed wording

- “This is developmental monitoring, not a screening or diagnostic test.”
- “Your observations can help prepare a conversation with a health professional.”
- “If you have a concern, you do not need to wait for the next checklist.”
- “One observation is not a conclusion; development is discussed over time and in context.”

## Not allowed wording / claim limit

- Do not call the Milestones observation flow a screen, screening tool, test, assessment,
  evaluation, or clinical surveillance visit.
- Do not produce a score, threshold, risk category, pass/fail result, “screened positive”,
  “screened negative”, “normal”, “delayed”, “behind”, or “advanced”.
- Do not claim that completing all checklist items rules out a developmental condition or removes
  the need to discuss a caregiver concern.
- Do not treat a single missed item as evidence of a disorder or automatically prescribe a
  referral, therapy, or diagnosis.
- Do not imply that the CDC milestone threshold is a prediction, percentile, test cutoff, or
  statement about an individual child’s potential.
- Claim limit: supports longitudinal observation and conversation preparation only; it does not
  screen, diagnose, determine eligibility, or replace a validated professional process.

## Safety note

A caregiver concern, loss of a previously observed skill, or professional concern should be
discussed promptly through the product’s existing safety guidance rather than postponed until a
scheduled app check. Urgent health or safety concerns require appropriate local medical help.
