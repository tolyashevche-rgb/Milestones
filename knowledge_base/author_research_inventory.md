# Author Research Inventory for Recommendation Base

Version: 0.1.0
Date: 2026-06-01

## Purpose

This file keeps the full author/source inventory for the Milestones recommendation layer.

The product should stay evidence-first:

1. CDC / AAP / WHO define milestones, safety boundaries, monitoring language and when to ask a professional.
2. Research authors explain mechanisms: language, play, attachment, regulation, brain development, motor development.
3. Pedagogical or method authors inspire activities, environment and parent routines, but do not override guideline safety.

## Evidence Roles

| Role | Meaning | Allowed use |
|---|---|---|
| guideline | Official guidance or clinical/public-health framework | Milestones, discuss/referral logic, safety language |
| research | Academic or evidence-informed developmental work | Mechanisms, explanations, source-backed claims |
| expert_model | Author framework useful for parent-friendly recommendations | Explanatory models and practical guidance |
| pedagogy_method | Educational method or tradition | Activity inspiration, environment, routines |
| practice_secondary | Practical or secondary source | Supportive ideas only, not strong claims |
| needs_verification | Name/work needs confirmation | Do not ingest until verified |

## Notes For Milestones Project

- CDC/AAP/WHO remain the source of truth for milestones and discuss/referral logic.
- Authors can enrich the recommendation layer but must not create diagnosis, delay labels, or guarantees.
- Shichida, Ibuka, Suzuki and similar method authors should be used as inspiration, not proof.
- Doman belongs in the same cautious method bucket: inspiration only, never pressure, claims, scoring or proof.
- Luria is useful for internal theoretical grounding, but parent-facing text should translate it into simple, non-clinical language.
- Bernstein is useful for internal motor-control grounding, but parent-facing text should translate it into simple movement/play language.
- Harvard Center on the Developing Child should be treated as a Silver mechanism source: practical science translation for why responsive interaction, play and executive-function routines support development. It does not define milestones or replace CDC/AAP/WHO safety boundaries.
- Frances Page Glascoe is especially relevant because the project deals with parent concerns, surveillance, screening-adjacent language and professional summaries.
