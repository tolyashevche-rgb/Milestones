# Parent Test Script: Stage 4 Prototype

> **Archived starting point.** For the current Stage 5 UA build, use the ready-to-run
> Ukrainian facilitator pack: [parent_test_facilitator_pack_ua.md](parent_test_facilitator_pack_ua.md).

Version: Stage 4 draft
Date: 2026-05-05

## Test Goal

Validate whether parents understand the product concept, feel calmer rather than more anxious, and can complete the core flow:

1. choose baby's age;
2. review milestones;
3. open one activity;
4. understand `why it works`;
5. find when to discuss with a professional;
6. export or understand the pediatric summary.

## Target Testers

Minimum first round:

- 5 parents with babies 0-12 months;
- at least 2 parents living outside their home country;
- at least 1 parent who self-identifies as anxious about milestones;
- at least 1 parent who prefers practical play ideas over reading.

## Setup

Show the static prototype:

`prototype_stage4/legacy-reference.html` (historical script only)

Ask the tester to think out loud. Do not explain the product before they use it.

## Opening Prompt

> Imagine your baby is around 6 months old. You are wondering what to notice, what to do at home and whether anything is worth asking your pediatrician about. Please use this page as you naturally would.

## Tasks

### Task 1: First Impression

Ask:

- What do you think this product does?
- Who is it for?
- Does the page feel calm, medical, salesy, or confusing?

Success signal:

- Parent says some version of: "It helps me know what to observe, what play to do, and when to ask."

### Task 2: Milestone Check

Ask:

- Find the milestones for 6 months.
- Mark two milestones as observed.
- Mark one as "not sure".

Observe:

- Can parent find state controls?
- Does "not sure" feel safe or alarming?

### Task 3: Activity

Ask:

- Open an activity that feels realistic today.
- Tell me what you would do with your baby.
- Tell me why the product says this activity helps.

Observe:

- Are steps clear?
- Is duration believable?
- Does `why it works` feel useful or too academic?

### Task 4: Ask / Professional Summary

Ask:

- Where would you go if something worried you?
- What would you show a pediatrician?

Observe:

- Can parent find `Ask`?
- Does disclaimer feel reassuring or scary?
- Does summary feel useful?

### Task 5: Free Starter Pack CTA

Ask:

- Would you enter your email for the free 0-12 month starter pack?
- What would need to be included for it to feel worth downloading?

## Questions After Use

1. What part felt most useful?
2. What part felt unnecessary?
3. Did anything make you more anxious?
4. Did anything feel too medical?
5. Did anything feel like it was promising too much?
6. What would you expect in the paid version?
7. Would you share this with another parent?

## Rating Scales

Ask tester to rate 1-5:

- Clarity: I understand what this product does.
- Calm: I feel less anxious after using it.
- Usefulness: I found at least one thing I would do today.
- Trust: I understand where the guidance comes from.
- Safety: I understand this does not replace a professional.
- Willingness: I would download the free starter pack.

## Red Flags

Stop and revise if testers say:

- "This makes me feel my baby is behind."
- "This feels like a diagnosis."
- "I do not know what to do next."
- "The activity sounds unsafe."
- "This is just CDC copied into a nicer page."
- "I cannot tell why I should trust it."
