# Free Starter Pack Draft: 0-12 Months

Working title:

> 0-12 Months Milestone Starter Pack

Positioning:

> A calm, evidence-informed guide for parents: what to watch, what to play, and when to ask.

## Pack Structure

### Page 1: How To Use This Pack

Core message:

> Milestones are not an exam. They are conversation tools. Use them to notice, support and ask early if something concerns you.

Method:

1. Watch: notice one or two age-typical signs.
2. Play: try one short activity.
3. Ask: write down concerns for your healthcare professional.

### Page 2: 2 Months Snapshot

Watch:

- baby looks toward faces;
- baby makes sounds beyond crying;
- baby briefly lifts head during awake tummy time.

Play:

- Face-to-Face Pause;
- Copy Baby's Coos;
- Mini Tummy Moment.

Ask:

- Does baby react to loud sounds?
- Do both arms and legs move?
- Has baby lost any skill or response you previously saw?

### Page 3: 4 Months Snapshot

Watch:

- baby smiles to engage you;
- baby coos or makes sounds back;
- baby pushes onto forearms during tummy time.

Play:

- Smile-Copy Game;
- Coo Conversation;
- Forearm Tummy Play.

Ask:

- Does baby respond to voices and sounds?
- Does one side seem much less active?
- Is tummy time unusually difficult or distressing?

### Page 4: 6 Months Snapshot

Watch:

- baby recognizes familiar people;
- baby takes turns with sounds;
- baby reaches for a wanted toy;
- baby pushes up during tummy play.

Play:

- Sound Turn-Taking;
- Side Reach Toy;
- Straight-Arm Tummy Reach.

Ask:

- Is baby making sounds?
- Is baby reaching for objects?
- Are feeding cues, movement, or interaction concerning you?

### Page 5: 9 Months Snapshot

Watch:

- baby responds to name;
- baby babbles repeated sounds;
- baby searches for dropped or hidden objects;
- baby sits without support.

Play:

- Name Pause Game;
- Babble Copy;
- Drop-and-Find Game;
- Sit-and-Reach Play.

Ask:

- Does baby respond to name or sound?
- Does baby use gestures or reach to communicate?
- Is one side of the body less active?

### Page 6: 12 Months Snapshot

Watch:

- baby plays simple social games;
- baby waves or uses familiar gestures;
- baby puts objects into containers;
- baby pulls to stand or cruises with support.

Play:

- Pat-a-Cake Pause;
- Bye-Bye Routine;
- In-and-Out Container Play;
- Stable Pull-to-Stand Play.

Ask:

- Does baby use gestures?
- Does baby make sounds or use a special name for caregiver?
- Are standing, feeding, or hand-use skills concerning you?

### Page 7: Pediatric Visit Notes

Template:

```text
Baby's age:
What I noticed:
What I have not seen yet:
Activities we tried:
My concerns:
Questions for the professional:
1.
2.
3.
```

Use:

- calm;
- practical;
- source-backed;

- guilt;
- guaranteed outcomes;
- treatment claims.
